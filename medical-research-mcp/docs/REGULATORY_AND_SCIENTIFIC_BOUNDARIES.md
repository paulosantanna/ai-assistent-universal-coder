# REGULATORY_AND_SCIENTIFIC_BOUNDARIES.md

## 1. Research-only boundary

This package supports software engineering, evidence organization, model development, computational simulation, and research planning.

It does not establish:

- clinical safety;
- clinical efficacy;
- analytical validity;
- clinical validity;
- regulatory clearance or approval;
- a cure.

## 2. Evidence hierarchy

These states must remain separate:

```text
Source discovery
≠ screened evidence
≠ extracted evidence
≠ validated evidence
≠ model prediction
≠ computational simulation
≠ retrospective validation
≠ external validation
≠ preclinical evidence
≠ clinical evidence
≠ replicated evidence of cure
```

## 3. Preclinical boundary

AEOS may prepare:

- evidence dossiers;
- assumptions;
- computational hypotheses;
- validation questions;
- traceability;
- statistical analysis plans for qualified review.

Execution requires qualified institutions and applicable scientific, biosafety, ethical, legal, and quality-system controls.

## 4. Human-research boundary

Before participant recruitment, applicable requirements may include:

- qualified principal investigator;
- ethics committee or IRB approval;
- regulatory authorization;
- public trial registration;
- informed consent;
- data safety monitoring;
- validated protocol;
- privacy and data-protection controls;
- adverse-event procedures;
- independent statistical oversight.

AEOS may not autonomously recruit, dose, intervene, diagnose, or recommend treatment.

## 5. Adaptive and continuous-learning boundary

Continuous learning must be controlled through:

- immutable versions;
- representative replay;
- shadow evaluation;
- predefined metrics;
- drift monitoring;
- independent review;
- approval;
- rollback;
- documented change control.

The system may not autonomously alter clinical behavior.

## 6. Transparency

Documentation must disclose:

- intended use;
- prohibited use;
- data sources;
- limitations;
- subgroup coverage;
- uncertainty;
- known failure modes;
- external validation status;
- model and dataset versions;
- update process;
- human oversight.

## 7. Cure claims

A computationally generated candidate may be called:

- hypothesis;
- target;
- candidate intervention;
- simulated result;
- evidence-supported research direction.

It must not be called a cure unless supported by appropriate replicated scientific and clinical evidence and qualified external review.
